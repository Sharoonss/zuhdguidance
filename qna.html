Do we attribute a place to Allah?
In Islamic theology, Allah is not confined to any place or direction. Allah exists without being in a place, as He existed before creating space and place. The concept of place applies to created beings, not to the Creator. Allah is above His creation in status and rank, not in physical location.
Is it permissible to name our child 'Abrar'?
Yes, 'Abrar' is a beautiful and permissible name in Islam. It means "the righteous ones" or "the pious ones" and is mentioned in the Quran. It's a plural form of 'Barr' meaning righteous, and choosing such names with good meanings is encouraged in Islam.
What is the blood expiation when in Ihram?
When in the state of Ihram, if a person violates certain prohibitions, they may need to offer Dam (blood expiation). This typically involves sacrificing a sheep or goat, or alternatively, fasting for three days during Hajj and seven days upon returning home, or feeding six poor people. The specific expiation depends on the nature of the violation.
Can I combine prayers while travelling?
Yes, Islam allows combining prayers (Jam' bayn as-Salatayn) while traveling. You can combine Dhuhr with Asr, and Maghrib with Isha. This can be done either by praying both at the time of the earlier prayer (Jam' Taqdeem) or at the time of the later prayer (Jam' Ta'kheer). This concession is given to ease the hardship of travel.
Is reciting Fatihah sufficient in prayer?
Reciting Al-Fatihah is obligatory in every unit (rak'ah) of prayer. However, it's recommended (sunnah) to recite additional verses from the Quran after Al-Fatihah, especially in the first two rak'ahs of Fajr, Maghrib, and Isha prayers, and in all rak'ahs of Dhuhr and Asr according to some scholars. The Prophet (peace be upon him) regularly recited additional verses after Al-Fatihah.
When is making hijrah obligatory?
Hijrah (migration) becomes obligatory when a Muslim cannot practice their religion freely and safely in their current location due to persecution or oppression. If staying would compromise one's faith, safety, or ability to fulfill religious obligations, and there's a safe place to migrate to, then hijrah may become a religious duty. However, each situation requires careful consideration of individual circumstances.
